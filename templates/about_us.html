About Maia

The Vision

We believe everyone should not be deprieved of their happiness. We all deserve happiness in the way we perceive happiness us. Unfortunately, due to circumstances we have found ourselves in, many of us do not remember when we last smiled with the very person whom we once loved. Our vision is to create a safe space for many who have find themselves on this journey, either at the beginning, being going through it for some time, or have com to a decision to make a very hard decision to not endure this any further.

The meaning of Maia is mother, and pronounced as "My-ah". Mothers are known to be caring, loving, kind, compassionate, protectors, filled with love and wisdom, but not just that alone. They are that and very courageous and strong. As women, with or without a child, we bare these characteristics and hence this page bared the name Maia. Our hope is that one through this platform, you will gain a sense of support and remember that you are not and do not have to ride these tides alone.

There is a community of sisters, of friends, of families out there who have rode the wave in front of you and some who will want to hear your story of how you overcame. We are here to listen, share,advice and or be a friends to you at this time in your life.

Before we can come closer to resolving an issue, we must first identify that there is an issue.


It is still an abuse if.. incidents of physical abuse seem minor when compared to those you have read about, seen on television, or heard other people talk about. There isn’t a “better” or “worse” form of physical abuse; severe injuries can result from being pushed, for example.
The incidents of physical abuse have only occurred one or two times in the relationship. Studies indicate that if your spouse/partner has injured you once, it is likely that the person will continue to physically assault you.
The physical assaults stopped when you became passive and gave up your right to express yourself as you desire, to move about freely and see others, and to make decisions. It is not a victory if you have to give up your rights as a person and a partner in exchange for ending the assault!
Physical violence has not occurred. The majority are emotionally and verbally assaulted. This can be just as frightening and is often more confusing to try to understand.
